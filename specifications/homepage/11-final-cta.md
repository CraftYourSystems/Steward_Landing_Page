# Purpose

The Final Call to Action concludes the homepage by inviting visitors to begin a conversation with Steward.

It is not intended to pressure visitors into making a purchase.

Its purpose is to provide a clear, confident next step after they have understood the product.

The section should feel like an invitation rather than a sales pitch.

---

# User Question

"What's my next step?"

---

# Business Goal

- Encourage demo bookings.
- Convert interest into meaningful conversations.
- Reinforce trust built throughout the homepage.
- End the experience with confidence and clarity.

---

# Core Message

The best way to understand Steward is to see how it fits your restaurant.

---

# Content

## Eyebrow

Ready to Explore Steward?

---

## Heading

Let's see how Steward fits your restaurant.

## Supporting Copy

Every restaurant operates differently.

Book a personalized demo, and we'll walk through your workflow, understand your operations, and show how Steward can support your team.

No pressure.

Just a conversation.

# Primary CTA

Book a Demo

Navigates to the demo booking page or contact form.

---

# Secondary CTA

Contact Us

For visitors who prefer to start with a conversation instead of scheduling a demo.

---

# Supporting Note

No commitments.

No hidden costs.

We'll simply understand your restaurant and answer your questions.

---

# Layout

Centered.

Large heading.

Supporting copy.

CTA button group.

Supporting note below the buttons.

Maximum content width approximately 700px.

Generous whitespace.

This section should feel calm and spacious.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

1. Heading

2. Primary CTA

3. Supporting Copy

4. Secondary CTA

5. Supporting Note

The primary CTA should be clearly visible without overwhelming the section.

---

# Visual Style

Editorial.

Minimal.

Bright background.

Large typography.

Soft button styling.

No decorative illustrations.

No unnecessary graphics.

Whitespace should create emphasis.

---

# Motion

Section fades into view.

Heading appears first.

Supporting copy follows.

Buttons appear last.

Hover effects remain subtle.

No dramatic entrance animations.

No pulsing buttons.

No attention-seeking effects.

---

# Interaction

Primary CTA

Navigates to demo booking.

Secondary CTA

Navigates to the contact page.

Hover

Subtle elevation.

Small opacity transition.

Focus

Visible keyboard focus.

Accessible interactions throughout.

---

# Accessibility

Single logical heading.

Keyboard-accessible buttons.

High contrast.

Readable typography.

Touch-friendly button sizes.

Clear focus indicators.

---

# Copy Principles

Invite.

Do not pressure.

Avoid:

"Start Now"

"Limited Time"

"Don't Miss Out"

"Claim Your Spot"

"Only Today"

Use language that encourages conversation rather than urgency.

---

# Voice

Warm.

Professional.

Confident.

Respectful.

Never pushy.

Never desperate.

Never overly enthusiastic.

---

# Engineering Notes

Reusable CTA component.

Responsive button group.

Configurable destinations.

Optimized spacing for all breakpoints.

Future analytics events should be attachable without layout changes.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Visitors should understand:

- The next step is a personalized demo.
- Steward values understanding their restaurant before recommending anything.
- There is no pressure or obligation.
- Contacting Steward is simple and approachable.

The section should naturally encourage action after trust has been established.

---

# Out of Scope

Countdown timers.

Limited-time offers.

Discount banners.

Exit-intent popups.

Newsletter signups.

Referral campaigns.

Marketing promotions.

These intentionally remain outside Version 1.

---

# Definition of Done

The Final CTA is complete when visitors naturally think:

"I'm ready to have a conversation."

The section succeeds when it turns confidence into curiosity, rather than curiosity into pressure.
