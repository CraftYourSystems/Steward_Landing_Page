Version: 1.0

Status: Ready for Implementation

References

---

# Purpose

The Needle section introduces Steward's operational philosophy through experience rather than explanation.

Instead of describing Needle, the section allows visitors to experience how it communicates.

Its purpose is to establish trust.

Needle should feel like an experienced restaurant manager who has already reviewed the restaurant before the owner arrives.

It should reassure.

Not impress.

---

# User Question

"Why should I trust Steward?"

---

# Business Goal

- Differentiate Steward from traditional restaurant software.
- Introduce Needle naturally.
- Establish operational credibility.
- Build confidence before showing the dashboard.

---

# Core Message

Needle quietly understands the operational state of your restaurant.

It communicates only what matters.

Nothing more.

Nothing less.

---

# Content

## Eyebrow

Meet Needle

---

## Heading

Your restaurant's operational voice.

---

## Supporting Copy

Needle reviews what Steward already knows and prepares a clear operational briefing before your day begins.

It communicates facts, not assumptions.

It exists to help you understand your restaurant with confidence.

---

# Primary Component

Editorial briefing card.

---

Example

Good morning.

QR Ordering is active.

Kitchen Display is online.

Payments are connected.

No pending orders.

You're ready for today's service.

Prepared at 8:42 AM

Open Needle →

---

The content should feel calm.

Never dramatic.

Never conversational.

Never promotional.

---

# Secondary Content

Small explanatory paragraph below the card.

Example:

Needle doesn't replace restaurant owners.

It simply helps them begin every day with clarity.

---

# Layout

Centered section.

Large heading.

Editorial spacing.

Briefing card positioned directly below.

Supporting explanation beneath the card.

Maximum reading width.

Large whitespace.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

1. Needle briefing card

2. Heading

3. Supporting paragraph

4. Open Needle link

The card should naturally become the focal point.

---

# Visual Style

White editorial card.

Rounded corners.

Soft border.

Minimal shadow.

Large serif greeting.

Smaller sans-serif operational items.

Timestamp uses subdued typography.

Everything should feel premium.

Nothing should feel futuristic.

---

# Motion

Section fades into view.

Card rises subtly.

Individual briefing lines appear with slight stagger.

Animations remain understated.

No typing effects.

No AI chat animations.

No blinking cursors.

No loading simulations.

Needle should already feel prepared.

---

# Interaction

Open Needle

Hover:

Subtle underline or opacity transition.

Click:

Reserved for future product experience.

For Version 1 it may simply navigate to the demo section or remain inactive.

No fake interactions.

---

# Accessibility

Semantic headings.

Proper reading order.

High contrast.

Keyboard accessible link.

Screen readers should read the briefing naturally.

---

# Copy Principles

Needle communicates:

Facts.

Current operational status.

Prepared information.

Never:

Predictions.

Marketing.

Compliments.

Speculation.

Conversational filler.

---

# Voice

Needle sounds like:

An experienced operations manager.

Never like:

A chatbot.

An AI assistant.

A motivational coach.

A customer support representative.

---

Acceptable

Kitchen Display is online.

Payments are synchronized.

17 orders completed.

Lunch service begins in 23 minutes.

Prepared at 8:42 AM.

---

Avoid

Everything looks amazing today!

Great work!

You're crushing it!

I think...

Maybe...

You should probably...

---

# Engineering Notes

Briefing card built as reusable component.

Operational rows individually reusable.

Timestamp isolated.

Animations controlled with Framer Motion.

Content should support future API integration.

No hardcoded visual assumptions beyond placeholder operational data.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Visitors should immediately understand:

Needle is not a chatbot.

Needle communicates operational truth.

Needle feels trustworthy.

Needle belongs inside a real operating system.

The section should reduce uncertainty rather than create excitement.

---

# Out of Scope

Chat interface.

Conversation history.

Prompt input.

AI avatars.

Voice assistant.

Typing indicators.

Streaming text.

Generative conversations.

These are intentionally excluded.

---

# Definition of Done

The Needle section is complete when visitors naturally believe:

"This feels like software that already understands what's happening in my restaurant."

The section succeeds when trust is created without making extraordinary claims.
