# Homepage Architecture

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

References:
- Steward Constitution

---

# Purpose

The Steward homepage exists to introduce the product with honesty, clarity, and confidence.

Its objective is not to maximize excitement or showcase every feature.

Its purpose is to help restaurant owners understand what Steward is, why it exists, and why they should trust it.

The homepage should feel calm, structured, and intentional.

Every section contributes to a single narrative.

---

# Primary Audience

Primary

Restaurant owners.

Secondary

Restaurant managers.

Future audiences such as investors, partners, and developers are considered secondary and should not influence the homepage narrative.

---

# Homepage Objective

When a visitor leaves the homepage they should understand:

- What Steward is.
- Why it exists.
- How it helps restaurants.
- Why they should trust it.
- What to do next.

Nothing more.

Nothing less.

---

# Core Narrative

The homepage follows a story rather than a marketing funnel.

Each section answers exactly one question.

The visitor should never feel overwhelmed with information.

The narrative gradually builds confidence.

# Information Hierarchy

Every section should introduce only one major idea.

Visitors should never need to process multiple unrelated concepts simultaneously.

The homepage progresses from broad understanding to detailed confidence.

---

# Emotional Journey

The homepage should guide visitors through the following emotional progression.

Curiosity

↓

Understanding

↓

Trust

↓

Confidence

↓

Action

Excitement is not the objective.

Confidence is.

---

# Visual Rhythm

Every section should have:

- One primary focal point.
- Clear hierarchy.
- Generous whitespace.
- Predictable spacing.
- Calm pacing.

No section should visually compete with the previous one.

---

# Motion Philosophy

Motion should reinforce continuity.

Animation should never become the primary visual element.

Every transition should support understanding.

---

# Copy Philosophy

The homepage should:

Explain.

Not persuade.

Demonstrate.

Not exaggerate.

Teach.

Not advertise.

Every sentence should strengthen trust.

---

# Product Truth

The homepage must never claim capabilities Steward does not currently possess.

Future capabilities must be clearly separated from production functionality.

Authenticity always takes priority over marketing.

---

# Success Criteria

The homepage is successful when a restaurant owner can answer the following questions after reading it:

- What is Steward?
- Why should I trust it?
- How would it fit into my restaurant?
- Why is it different?
- What should I do next?

If these questions remain unanswered, the homepage architecture has failed.

---

# Out of Scope

The homepage is not intended to:

- Demonstrate every feature.
- Replace a product demo.
- Teach software usage.
- Document technical architecture.
- Serve as product documentation.

Its responsibility is to introduce Steward with clarity and honesty.

---

# Relationship to Specifications

Every homepage specification inherits from this document.

Individual sections may define their own layouts, interactions, and content, but they must never contradict the narrative, principles, or structure established here.

This document is the architectural blueprint for the entire homepage.
