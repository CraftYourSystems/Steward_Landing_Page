# Purpose

This section demonstrates how Steward fits naturally into a restaurant owner's day.

Instead of explaining features, it illustrates a typical day where Steward quietly supports operations from opening to closing.

The visitor should imagine spending less time managing systems and more time running the restaurant.

---

# User Question

"What would using Steward actually feel like?"

---

# Business Goal

- Humanize the product.
- Show operational continuity.
- Reinforce Steward's role as a quiet partner.
- Help visitors envision Steward in their own restaurant.

---

# Core Message

Steward works throughout the day so restaurant owners can focus on their restaurant—not their software.

---

# Content

## Eyebrow

A Day with Steward

## Heading

From opening to closing, Steward works quietly in the background.

## Supporting Copy

Every service brings hundreds of small operational decisions.

# Primary Component

Daily Timeline

The timeline represents a complete restaurant day.

---

### Morning

**Opening Brief**

Needle prepares the day's operational summary before service begins.

• Staff ready

### Service Begins

**Orders Flow Naturally**

Customers scan, order, and payments begin flowing while the kitchen receives orders instantly.

---

### Lunch Rush

**Operations Stay Connected**

---

# Layout

Centered section.

Timeline occupies the majority of the space.

Alternating timeline cards on desktop.

Single-column timeline on mobile.

Generous spacing between events.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

1. Timeline

2. Heading

3. Supporting Copy

Timeline entries should naturally guide the reader downward.

---

# Visual Style

Editorial.

Minimal.

Soft timeline line.

Simple circular milestones.

Small supporting icons if appropriate.

No decorative illustrations.

No exaggerated graphics.

The timeline should feel calm and readable.

---

# Motion

Timeline entries appear progressively as they enter the viewport.

Each milestone fades upward gently.

The timeline line itself appears naturally.

No continuous animations.

No autoplay.

No distracting effects.

---

# Interaction

Hover

Slight elevation.

Optional subtle highlight.

No hidden content.

The experience should remain fully understandable without interaction.

---

# Accessibility

Semantic timeline structure.

Keyboard accessible cards.

Logical reading order.

Animations respect reduced-motion preferences.

High contrast throughout.

---

# Copy Principles

Focus on outcomes.

Avoid technical implementation.

Describe restaurant operations using familiar language.

Never imply Steward replaces restaurant staff.

Never exaggerate automation.

---

# Voice

Calm.

Confident.

Operational.

Human.

Never promotional.

Never conversational.

Never overly emotional.

---

# Engineering Notes

Reusable timeline component.

Responsive vertical layout.

Intersection Observer or Framer Motion for entry animations.

Cards should support future content updates without structural changes.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Visitors should understand:

- Steward supports the entire operating day.
- The product works continuously without demanding attention.
- Needle provides clarity at both the beginning and end of the day.
- Restaurant owners remain focused on people rather than software.

The section should help visitors picture Steward inside their own daily routine.

---

# Out of Scope

Customer testimonials.

Performance statistics.

Feature comparisons.

Operational metrics.

Analytics dashboards.

Complex animations.

These belong in later sections or inside the product.

---

# Definition of Done

The section is complete when visitors naturally think:

"I can imagine running my restaurant like this."

Rather than explaining Steward, the timeline should help visitors experience what working with Steward would feel like.
