# Homepage Specification

## 09 — Early Partners

Version: 1.0

Status: Ready for Implementation

References

- Steward Constitution
- Homepage Architecture
- Product Truth

---

# Purpose

The Early Partners section introduces the restaurants and businesses working with Steward during its early journey.

Rather than showcasing testimonials or inflated customer numbers, this section demonstrates that Steward is being developed alongside real restaurant operators.

Its purpose is to build trust through authenticity.

---

# User Question

"Who is already using or helping build Steward?"

---

# Business Goal

- Establish credibility.
- Demonstrate real-world collaboration.
- Reinforce Product Truth.
- Show that Steward is being refined with operational feedback.

---

# Core Message

Steward is growing alongside restaurants—not in isolation.

Every partnership helps shape a better operating system.

---

# Content

## Eyebrow

Growing Together

---

## Heading

Built with restaurants.

Improved through every partnership.

---

## Supporting Copy

Every restaurant operates differently.

By working closely with our early partners, Steward continues to evolve around real operational needs instead of assumptions.

---

# Primary Component

Partner Cards

Each card represents an early partner.

Card includes:

- Restaurant logo (if available)
- Restaurant name
- City
- Status

Example

Venna Café

Hyderabad

Early Partner

---

Optional small note

"Helping shape Steward's first release."

---

If multiple partners exist

Display them in a responsive grid.

If only one partner exists

Center a single featured partner card.

Do not create placeholder partners.

---

# Secondary Component

Timeline

A simple milestone row beneath the cards.

Example

✓ First Restaurant Partner

↓

✓ First Live Deployment

↓

✓ Multi-Branch Support

↓

Future Partnerships

The timeline should communicate progress—not scale.

---

# Layout

Centered.

Heading.

Supporting Copy.

Partner Cards.

Progress Timeline.

Large whitespace throughout.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

1. Partner Cards

2. Heading

3. Timeline

4. Supporting Copy

The partners themselves should receive the most attention.

---

# Visual Style

Minimal.

Editorial.

Soft borders.

Rounded corners.

Simple typography.

Restaurant logos should remain monochromatic where possible.

No oversized branding.

No marketing badges.

---

# Motion

Cards fade upward.

Small stagger.

Timeline appears after the cards.

Animations remain subtle.

No counters.

No celebratory effects.

No confetti.

---

# Interaction

Hover

Soft elevation.

Optional border emphasis.

Click

May navigate to a future case study.

Version 1 may remain static.

---

# Accessibility

Partner information readable by screen readers.

Meaningful alt text for logos.

Keyboard accessible if cards become interactive.

Responsive layout across all breakpoints.

---

# Copy Principles

Communicate partnership.

Avoid exaggeration.

Never imply market leadership.

Never imply widespread adoption.

Never fabricate customer numbers.

Use language such as:

Early Partner

Pilot Restaurant

Launch Partner

Operational Collaboration

Avoid language such as:

Trusted by thousands.

Industry leader.

Market-leading platform.

Fastest growing.

# Voice

Grateful.

Professional.

Confident.

Honest.

The section should celebrate collaboration—not popularity.

---

# Engineering Notes

Reusable partner card component.

Support optional logo.

Responsive grid.

Future partners should be configurable through content only.

Timeline component reusable.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Visitors should understand:

- Steward is already working with real restaurants.
- The product is improving through practical feedback.
- Growth is intentional and transparent.
- Steward values long-term partnerships over rapid expansion claims.

The section should increase confidence through authenticity.

---

# Out of Scope

Testimonials.

Star ratings.

Review scores.

Case studies.

Revenue numbers.

User counters.

Maps showing hundreds of locations.

Awards.

Certifications.

These should only appear once they genuinely exist.

---

# Definition of Done

The Early Partners section is complete when visitors naturally think:

"Steward is still early—but it's already being built with real restaurants."

The section should inspire confidence by showing honest progress rather than manufactured popularity.
