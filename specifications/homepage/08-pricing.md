# Purpose

The Pricing section helps restaurant owners understand how Steward is offered and what they receive.

It should make pricing feel transparent, predictable, and fair.

The emphasis is on clarity rather than persuasion.

Visitors should leave knowing exactly what to expect.

---

# User Question

"What does Steward cost?"

---

# Business Goal

- Present pricing clearly.
- Reduce purchase hesitation.
- Encourage demo bookings.
- Reinforce trust through transparency.

# Pricing Display

If prices are finalized:

Display them prominently.

If pricing is still under discussion:

Display

"Custom pricing based on your restaurant."

Never display placeholder currency values.

Never fabricate discounts.

---

# Layout

Centered.

Heading.

Supporting copy.

Three pricing cards.

Cards should have equal visual weight.

Recommended plan may receive subtle emphasis.

Never dominate the section.

---

# Visual Hierarchy

1. Pricing Cards

2. Heading

3. Supporting Copy

4. CTA Buttons

---

# Visual Style

Minimal.

Editorial.

Generous spacing.

Soft borders.

Rounded corners.

No loud pricing badges.

No countdown timers.

No discount stickers.

No flashing highlights.

---

# Motion

Cards fade into view.

Small upward transition.

Very subtle hover elevation.

No card flipping.

No animated pricing.

No attention-grabbing effects.

---

# Interaction

Hover

Soft elevation.

Border emphasis.

Click

CTA navigates to demo booking.

No expandable pricing tables.

No hidden fees revealed later.

---

# Accessibility

Keyboard accessible cards.

Buttons with descriptive labels.

Readable typography.

High color contrast.

Logical heading structure.

---

# Copy Principles

Pricing should communicate:

Transparency.

Fairness.

Predictability.

Avoid:

"Limited-time offer"

"Act now"

"Only today"

"Best value"

"Guaranteed ROI"

Artificial urgency is prohibited.

---

# Voice

Honest.

Professional.

Calm.

Confident.

Never aggressive.

Never sales-driven.

---

# Engineering Notes

Reusable pricing card component.

Responsive grid.

Support optional highlighted plan.

Pricing values configurable.

Future billing models should require only content updates.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Visitors should understand:

- Steward offers straightforward pricing.
- Plans scale with restaurant needs.
- Demo booking is the natural next step.
- There are no hidden surprises.

The pricing section should build confidence rather than create pressure.

---

# Out of Scope

Annual billing calculators.

Monthly savings animations.

Discount campaigns.

Coupon codes.

Referral incentives.

Free trial countdowns.

Complex pricing matrices.

These belong in future marketing campaigns, not Version 1.

---

# Definition of Done

The Pricing section is complete when visitors naturally think:

"I understand how Steward is priced, and I know what the next step is."

The goal is clarity—not persuasion.
